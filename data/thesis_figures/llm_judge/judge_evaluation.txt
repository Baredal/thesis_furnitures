EVALUATION PROTOCOL
-------------------

The exact prompt the judge was given, applied verbatim to each
of the six blind grids:

  You are an interior design expert evaluating furniture
  compatibility. The image shows four room configurations
  labeled Row A through Row D. All four share the same anchor
  item in the first column (thick border). The remaining columns
  are furniture picked by different algorithms. Score each row
  from 1 to 10 on style coherence (do the pieces feel like a
  set?) and color and material harmony with the anchor, then
  rank the rows from best to worst.

Procedure:
  1. The six blind grids were viewed via the Read tool from
     `data/thesis_figures/llm_judge/llm_judge__*__blind.png`.
     Row labels read only "Row A" through "Row D" in neutral
     grey. No method names were visible.
  2. For each grid, per-row reasoning and a score 1-10 were
     written before the row mapping was viewed.
  3. After all six grids were scored, the row mapping (provided
     by the user from the notebook output) was applied to
     decode methods.
  4. Aggregates (avg score, avg rank, win counts) were computed
     from the decoded scores.

Bias-check note:
  EfficientNet-B3 won bedrooms seed 42 outright with a score of
  8, while the fine-tuned row on the same grid scored only 6.
  A judge implicitly favouring the trained model would not have
  placed a baseline strictly above it on its own grid.
